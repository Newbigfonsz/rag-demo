# The Tiger (虎 - Hǔ)

## Overview
The Tiger is the third animal in the Chinese zodiac cycle. People born in the Year of the Tiger are known for their bravery, competitiveness, and unpredictability. The Tiger is considered the king of all beasts in Chinese culture, symbolizing power and respect.

## Years of the Tiger
- 1926, 1938, 1950, 1962, 1974, 1986, 1998, 2010, 2022, 2034

## Core Personality Traits

### Positive Traits
- **Brave**: Courageous in the face of challenges
- **Confident**: Strong self-assurance and leadership presence
- **Competitive**: Driven to excel and win
- **Charismatic**: Natural ability to attract and inspire others
- **Passionate**: Intense enthusiasm for their interests
- **Generous**: Willing to help others and share resources

### Challenging Traits
- **Impulsive**: Acts without thinking through consequences
- **Arrogant**: Can be overconfident and dismissive
- **Short-tempered**: Quick to anger and confrontation
- **Restless**: Difficulty with routine and patience
- **Domineering**: Tendency to control situations and people

## Element Variations

### Wood Tiger (1914, 1974)
More compassionate and cooperative. Better team players. Still ambitious but more willing to share success. Artistic and creative tendencies.

### Fire Tiger (1926, 1986)
Extremely energetic and optimistic. Born leaders with intense personalities. Can be theatrical and dramatic. Highly independent and freedom-loving.

### Earth Tiger (1938, 1998)
More grounded and realistic. Better at long-term planning. Still adventurous but more practical. Takes responsibilities seriously.

### Metal Tiger (1950, 2010)
Most determined and assertive. Strong sense of competition. Can be aggressive in pursuing goals. High standards for themselves and others.

### Water Tiger (1962, 2022)
More intuitive and open-minded. Better at reading situations and people. Calm exterior hiding powerful emotions. More flexible in approach.

## Compatibility

### Most Compatible

### Least Compatible

- **Tiger**: Too similar; power struggles inevitable
- **Snake**: Mutual suspicion and misunderstanding
- **Monkey**: Competitive rivalry turns destructive
