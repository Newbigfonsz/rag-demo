# The Pig (猪 - Zhū)

## Overview
The Pig is the twelfth and final animal in the Chinese zodiac cycle. People born in the Year of the Pig are known for their generosity, compassion, and love of life's pleasures. The Pig symbolizes wealth, good fortune, and honesty in Chinese culture.

## Years of the Pig
- 1935, 1947, 1959, 1971, 1983, 1995, 2007, 2019, 2031, 2043

## Core Personality Traits

### Positive Traits
- **Generous**: Giving and selfless with others
- **Compassionate**: Deep empathy and kindness
- **Diligent**: Hardworking when motivated
- **Sincere**: Genuine and authentic in relationships
- **Sociable**: Enjoys company and entertainment
- **Calm**: Peaceful and non-confrontational

### Challenging Traits
- **Naive**: Can be too trusting of others
- **Materialistic**: Strong attachment to comfort
- **Indulgent**: Tendency toward excess
- **Lazy**: Can avoid hard work when possible
- **Gullible**: Easily deceived by manipulation

## Element Variations

### Wood Pig (1935, 1995)
More helpful and generous. Strong supporters of others. May be taken advantage of. Good-hearted and sincere.

### Fire Pig (1947, 2007)
Most ambitious and courageous. Natural leaders with strong convictions. Can be impatient. Bold and adventurous.

### Earth Pig (1959, 2019)
Most practical and punctual. Better at financial management. Reliable and hardworking. Strong social skills.

### Metal Pig (1911, 1971)
Most passionate and determined. Strong personality and opinions. Can be stubborn. Broad-minded and sociable.

### Water Pig (1923, 1983)
Most diplomatic and responsible. Better at reading situations. Trusting and gentle. Strong intuition.

## Compatibility

### Most Compatible

### Least Compatible
