# The Goat (羊 - Yáng)

## Overview
The Goat (also called Sheep or Ram) is the eighth animal in the Chinese zodiac cycle. People born in the Year of the Goat are known for their gentle nature, creativity, and compassion. The Goat symbolizes peace, harmony, and artistic expression in Chinese culture.

## Years of the Goat
- 1931, 1943, 1955, 1967, 1979, 1991, 2003, 2015, 2027, 2039

## Core Personality Traits

### Positive Traits
- **Gentle**: Kind and considerate in all dealings
- **Creative**: Strong artistic abilities and imagination
- **Compassionate**: Deep empathy for others
- **Elegant**: Refined taste and appreciation for beauty
- **Calm**: Peaceful and non-confrontational
- **Resilient**: Inner strength despite gentle exterior

### Challenging Traits
- **Indecisive**: Difficulty making firm choices
- **Pessimistic**: Tendency toward worry and anxiety
- **Dependent**: May rely too heavily on others
- **Disorganized**: Struggles with practical matters
- **Sensitive**: Easily hurt by criticism

## Element Variations

### Wood Goat (1955, 2015)
More compassionate and helpful. Strong friendships. Can be taken advantage of. Appreciates nature and tradition.

### Fire Goat (1907, 1967)
More courageous and outgoing. Better at standing up for beliefs. Dramatic flair. Can be impulsive with finances.

### Earth Goat (1919, 1979)
Most practical and hardworking. More financially stable. Optimistic and fair-minded. Strong family values.

### Metal Goat (1931, 1991)
More determined and ambitious. Protective of loved ones. Strong artistic sensibilities. Can seem aloof.

### Water Goat (1943, 2003)
Most sensitive and intuitive. Appealing personality that attracts help. Can be too influenced by others. Strong need for security.

## Compatibility

### Most Compatible
- **Rabbit**: Harmonious and peaceful partnership

- **Pig**: Understanding and nurturing relationship

### Least Compatible
- **Ox**: Ox finds Goat impractical and dreamy
- **Dog**: Difficulty understanding each other's values
- **Rat**: Different approaches to life and security
