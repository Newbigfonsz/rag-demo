# The Ox (牛 - Niú)

## Overview
The Ox is the second animal in the Chinese zodiac cycle. People born in the Year of the Ox are known for their diligence, dependability, and strength. The Ox symbolizes hard work and honesty in Chinese culture.

## Years of the Ox
- 1925, 1937, 1949, 1961, 1973, 1985, 1997, 2009, 2021, 2033

## Core Personality Traits

### Positive Traits
- **Diligent**: Hardworking and dedicated to their tasks
- **Dependable**: Extremely reliable and trustworthy
- **Patient**: Willing to work steadily toward long-term goals
- **Honest**: Values integrity and straightforward communication
- **Strong**: Both physically and mentally resilient
- **Methodical**: Approaches tasks with careful planning

### Challenging Traits
- **Stubborn**: Can be extremely inflexible and set in their ways
- **Conservative**: Resistant to change and new ideas
- **Demanding**: High expectations of themselves and others
- **Reserved**: Difficulty expressing emotions openly
- **Judgmental**: Can be critical of those who don't meet their standards

## Element Variations

### Wood Ox (1925, 1985)
More flexible and open-minded than other Oxen. Better at working with others. Still maintains strong work ethic. More comfortable expressing emotions.

### Fire Ox (1937, 1997)
More dynamic and short-tempered. Natural leaders with authority. Can be impersonal and less attentive to family. Driven to succeed at all costs.

### Earth Ox (1949, 2009)
The most grounded and practical. Extremely loyal to family and friends. Modest and unassuming. Patient wealth builders over time.

### Metal Ox (1961, 2021)
Most hardworking and determined. Very logical and straightforward. Can seem cold or unemotional. Fiercely dedicated to their goals.

### Water Ox (1973, 2033)
Most flexible and patient. Better at seeing others' perspectives. Strong sense of justice. More willing to adapt methods while maintaining goals.

## Compatibility

### Most Compatible
- **Rat**: Complementary partnership; shared values and goals
- **Snake**: Deep intellectual connection; mutual respect
- **Rooster**: Harmonious relationship; similar work ethics

### Least Compatible
- **Tiger**: Constant power struggles and conflicts
- **Dragon**: Both too strong-willed to compromise
- **Horse**: Fundamentally different approaches to life
- **Goat**: Ox finds Goat too dreamy and impractical

## Career Paths
Oxen excel in careers requiring persistence and reliability:
- Agriculture and farming
- Engineering and construction
- Medicine and healthcare
- Banking and finance
- Military and law enforcement
- Manufacturing and quality control
