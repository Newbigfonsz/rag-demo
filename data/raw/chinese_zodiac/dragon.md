# The Dragon (龙 - Lóng)

## Overview
The Dragon is the fifth animal in the Chinese zodiac cycle and the only mythical creature. People born in the Year of the Dragon are considered blessed and destined for greatness. The Dragon symbolizes power, nobility, honor, and success in Chinese culture.

## Years of the Dragon
- 1928, 1940, 1952, 1964, 1976, 1988, 2000, 2012, 2024, 2036

## Core Personality Traits

### Positive Traits
- **Ambitious**: Driven to achieve great things
- **Energetic**: Boundless vitality and enthusiasm
- **Confident**: Strong self-belief and presence
- **Intelligent**: Quick-minded and insightful
- **Charismatic**: Natural ability to lead and inspire
- **Fearless**: Willing to take risks others avoid

### Challenging Traits
- **Arrogant**: Can be condescending toward others
- **Demanding**: Expects perfection from themselves and others
- **Impatient**: Frustrated by slow progress
- **Aggressive**: Can be forceful in pursuing goals
- **Intolerant**: Difficulty accepting different viewpoints

## Element Variations

### Wood Dragon (1904, 1964)
More creative and cooperative. Open to others' opinions. Still ambitious but less domineering. Innovative problem solvers.

### Fire Dragon (1916, 1976)
Most intense and competitive. Natural leaders with magnetic personalities. Can be tyrannical. Extremely passionate about beliefs.

### Earth Dragon (1928, 1988)
More practical and grounded. Better at collaboration. Patient enough for long-term planning. Strong sense of justice.

### Metal Dragon (1940, 2000)
Most determined and inflexible. Strong principles and convictions. Natural integrity. Can be combative when challenged.

### Water Dragon (1952, 2012)
More flexible and diplomatic. Better at seeing different perspectives. Less ego-driven. Strategic and patient approach.

## Compatibility

### Most Compatible
- **Rat**: Mutual admiration and powerful partnership
- **Monkey**: Intellectual equals who challenge each other
- **Rooster**: Complementary strengths and shared ambition

### Least Compatible
- **Dog**: Fundamental value conflicts
- **Ox**: Both too stubborn to compromise
- **Dragon**: Power struggles and competition
- **Rabbit**: Dragon's intensity overwhelms Rabbit

- Politics and government

- Entrepreneurship
- Entertainment and performing arts
- Journalism and media
