# The Horse (马 - Mǎ)

## Overview
The Horse is the seventh animal in the Chinese zodiac cycle. People born in the Year of the Horse are known for their energy, independence, and free spirit. The Horse symbolizes speed, perseverance, and success in Chinese culture.

## Years of the Horse
- 1930, 1942, 1954, 1966, 1978, 1990, 2002, 2014, 2026, 2038

## Core Personality Traits

### Positive Traits
- **Energetic**: Abundant vitality and enthusiasm
- **Independent**: Values freedom and self-reliance
- **Adventurous**: Loves travel and new experiences
- **Cheerful**: Optimistic and uplifting presence
- **Quick-witted**: Fast thinking and adaptable
- **Sociable**: Enjoys company and makes friends easily

### Challenging Traits
- **Impatient**: Difficulty waiting or working slowly
- **Hot-headed**: Quick temper that cools fast
- **Self-centered**: Can neglect others' needs
- **Restless**: Struggles with routine and commitment
- **Irresponsible**: May abandon projects mid-way

## Element Variations

### Wood Horse (1954, 2014)
More grounded and systematic. Better at teamwork. Still adventurous but more stable. Imaginative and sentimental.

### Fire Horse (1906, 1966)
Most intense and passionate. Natural leaders but can be explosive. Strong opinions and convictions. Extremely independent.

### Earth Horse (1918, 1978)
More responsible and reliable. Better at long-term planning. Optimistic and helpful. Stronger decision-making abilities.

### Metal Horse (1930, 1990)
Most popular and committed in relationships. Straightforward and direct. Values freedom intensely. Strong work ethic when motivated.

### Water Horse (1942, 2002)
Most adaptable and flexible. Better at understanding emotions. Can be indecisive. Excellent communication skills.

## Compatibility

### Most Compatible
- **Tiger**: Adventurous partners who respect independence
- **Goat**: Complementary relationship with deep understanding
- **Dog**: Loyal and supportive partnership

### Least Compatible
- **Rat**: Fundamental conflicts in values and approach
- **Ox**: Too restrictive for Horse's free spirit
- **Horse**: Too similar; both need space and freedom
